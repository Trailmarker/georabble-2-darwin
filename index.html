## Digital regulatory ## workflow systems #### (DMIRS WA case study)
relating to systems I helped develop at the Department of Mines, Industry Regulation and Safety (DMIRS) in Western Australia from 2015 to 2017
down in the East Perth State government ghetto
### The Programme of Work Spatial Lodgement Portal PoW-S was launched by DMIRS in March 2017
PoW-S replaced existing multi-system and manual processes with a single online system used by mining companies to * propose exploration activities * get realtime feedback * streamline the provision of supporting information * fast-track "low impact" proposals
in its 2018 Annual Report, DMIRS stated * **2,643** Programme of Work applications for exploration had been processed * assessment times were cut by an average **7-10 days** * **93%** of users were _satisfied to very satisfied_ with PoW Spatial usability

#### The business drivers for PoW-S * reduce the time to assess MinEx proposals * improve the proposal process for industry users * reallocate staff and resources (eg to compliance)

many activities subject to regulation are inherently located in space: planning, mining, land use, environmental management

clear communication about regulating these activities can occur in a spatial view

**activities** and **regulatory datasets** are the inputs to business rules that generate the **encumbrances** for a proposal
#### Activities a proposed **activity** is a _spatial object_ * has a geography (point, line, polygon) * may have metadata affecting how it is processed
#### Activities eg: in PoW-S, **exploration ground disturbance activities** include drill holes and pads, tracks, costeans, campsites, core sample farms and geophysical surveys
#### Regulatory datasets * datasets maintained by a regulator or a partner agency * a determining role in regulatory activity * usually a spatial dataset (at DMIRS there were over 100 corporate spatial layers, about half were relevant to PoW-S)
#### Regulatory datasets * mining tenure and cadastral data * protected flora and fauna * national park boundaries * heritage and sacred sites * pastoral leases * rivers and bodies of water
#### Digital regulation: end to end
#### Digital regulation: proponents

Encumbrances

an encumbrance relates to a proposed activity and represents additional obligations

an encumbrance can also determine whether additional information must be supplied with a proposed activity

Encumbrances

native title document agreement with traditional owners
pastoral lease notify leaseholder
campsite near a watercourse define mitigation plan
#### Proponent supporting information Based on the proposal encumbrances, the proposal system generates a structured _online form_ so the proponent can supply supporting information for encumbrances, as text and attachments

Low impact proposals

what if all activities in a proposal have no impact?

it is a candidate for a "low impact" assessment which can be fast-tracked

#### Benefits for proponents 1. single consistent view of proposal 1. ETL proposal data from proponent systems 1. proposal can be edited in situ 1. instant view of (some) encumbrances on proposed activities 1. instant view of reasons for failure
#### Digital regulation: assessors
#### Benefits for assessors 1. integration of supporting data in a single view * legal fragments * levy and fee calculations * administrative data 1. "low impact" proposals can be fast-tracked 1. rendering of reports back to proponents is (partly) automatic
#### Assessment system example: EAVie (Environment Assessment VIEwer) * one-stop shop for DMIRS Environment Officers dealing with PoW-S proposals * encumbrances generate pro forma contract text * activities link to other systems of record via integrated services, displaying context * goal was to double the efficiency of EOs processing proposals

Phased approach

not everything needs to be built at once

PoW-S was one of several systems implemented by DMIRS for efficiency and rigour overseeing the mine life cycle, with others to follow for more complex proposal types

#### Take away points * digital regulatory workflow systems are spatial systems * based on detailed business analysis of all or some assessment factors * productivity wins for both external and internal use cases * huge process wins: transparency and unity
