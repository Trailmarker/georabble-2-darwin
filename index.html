### The Programme of Work Spatial Lodgement Portal PoW-S was launched by DMIRS in March 2017
PoW-S replaced old school manual processes with an online system used by mining companies to * propose exploration activities * get realtime feedback * streamline the provision of supporting information * fast-track "low impact" proposals
in its 2018 Annual Report, DMIRS stated * **2,643** Programme of Work applications for exploration had been processed * assessment times were cut by an average **7-10 days** * **93%** of users were _satisfied to very satisfied_ with PoW Spatial usability

#### The business drivers for PoW-S * reduce the time to assess MinEx proposals * improve the proposal process for industry users * reallocate staff and resources to compliance
**exploration ground disturbance activities** include drill holes and pads, tracks, costeans, campsites, core sample farms and surveys
an exploration activity is a _spatial object_, and it has * a geometry (point, line, polygon) * an envelope (buffered hull) * metadata which defines how it is processed PoW-S is about _entering exploration activities_
DMIRS maintains a geodatabase with layers sourced from different WA agencies * mining tenure and tenement data * protected flora and fauna * national parks and state reserves * heritage and sacred sites * pastoral leases * rivers and bodies of water about half of about 150 layers are relevant to PoW-S
#### Mining tenure * a proponent of MinEx activities needs to hold exploration tenure on the area to be disturbed * DMIRS has a demanding SLA on this commercially sensitive public tenure data * decisions impact the stock market
#### Encroachments in PoW-S, when a proposed activity intersects with a feature in a corporate layer, this is referred to as an _encroachment_ the geometry of an encroachment is fed into a PoW-S _rules engine_ which behaves differently based on the type of activity and the encroached layer
#### Encroachments 3 possible results of encroachment * no impact * activity must be removed or changed * activity causes an _encumbrance_ needing follow-up
#### Activities with no impact what if all activities in a proposal have no impact? it is a candidate for a "low impact" assessment which can be fast-tracked
#### Rejected activities PoW-S provides two options 1. remove the problem activity 2. _excise_ the encroachments (clip all intersecting layers from its envelope) in the old process, these problems would cause entire proposals to "bounce" and need to be resubmitted

Encroached and encumbered

native title document agreement with traditional owners
pastoral lease notify leaseholder
campsite near a watercourse define mitigation plan
once all rejected activities in a proposal are dealt with, PoW-S generates a wizard-style _questionnaire_ so the proponent can supply the supporting information for the proposal's outstanding encumbrances, as text and attachments
once the proposal is submitted and being processed for approval, encumbrances form part of the proponent's contractual obligations under any approved agreement
PoW-S was one of several spatial systems implemented by DMIRS for efficiency and rigour overseeing the mine life cycle, with others to follow
#### FRS or Feature Relationship Services * a "spatially enabled workflow management engine" * not an application, an API (no front end) * recorded a network view of relationships between audited groups of features passing through DMIRS processes: proposed, approved, actual
#### EAVie (Environment Assessment VIEwer) * one-stop shop for Environment Officers dealing with PoW-S proposals * encumbrances generate pro forma contract text * activities link to other systems of record via integrated services, displaying context * doubled the efficiency of EOs processing proposals
#### Technology (look away!) so PoW-S was built on ASP .NET MVC, SQL Server, ESRI ArcGIS Server, Swagger, Angular, Bootstrap, proj4js, ArcGIS JavaScript API, FME Server, ArcPy (but not ArcObjects), among others main driver for technology choices was incumbency and the available skill base the same concepts could be implemented with other technologies
#### Take away points * spatial workflow systems differ from other spatial systems * regulatory activity is focused forensically on data and legal matters * productivity gains from automating regulation can be huge
